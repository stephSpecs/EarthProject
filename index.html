What is Earth Day?

More About Earth Day!

  • First Earth Day was celebrated on April 22, 1970 in the United States.
  • Founder of Earth Day is Gaylord Nelson, a U.S. Senator from Wisconsin.
  • Purpose of Earth Day is to raise awareness about environmental issues and promote conservation efforts.
  • Global Participation Over 1 billion people in more than 190 countries participate in Earth Day activities each year.
  • Activities include tree planting, clean-up campaigns, educational events, and advocacy for environmental policies.
  • Important environmental laws, like the Clean Air Act, Clean Water Act, and Endangered Species Act, were influenced by the movement.
  • Earth Day helped inspire global agreements, like the Paris Climate Accord in 2015.
